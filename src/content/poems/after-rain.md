The street exhales  
after the storm,  
and every window keeps  
a little of the sky.

I touch the sill  
where water gathered,  
where the day left proof  
it had been heavy.

Some quiet things  
do not arrive as answers.

They come as silver beads,  
as leaf-breath,  
as the hush after thunder  
when the house remembers  
how to listen.
