The kettle sings  
like it has forgiven fire.

In the soft room,  
the cup warms my hands  
before the day can ask  
anything of them.

There is a kind of grace  
in not being interrupted.

A folded towel.  
A spoon returned.  
A flower keeping its shape  
long after bloom.

I have learned to count  
the smallest mercies first.
